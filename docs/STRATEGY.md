# Launch Strategy — Who To Target and How

## The Core Rule

**Local businesses are NOT the target.**
They compare custom development to $27 AI sites. They will never value it.

The right client needs something BUILT that templates and AI can't generate.

---

## Ideal Client Profile

| Client | What They Need | Why They Pay |
|---|---|---|
| Startup founder with an idea | MVP — app, SaaS, web system | Can't build it themselves |
| Business with a specific problem | Custom tool, internal system, automation | Off-the-shelf doesn't solve it |
| Company that outgrew their tools | Replace spreadsheets with real software | Real operational pain |
| Agency / consultancy | White-label dev work | They sell, you build |
| Someone who failed with templates | Tried Wix/Webflow, needs something real | Already knows they need a dev |

---

## What To Sell

Custom apps, SaaS products, and web systems.

Value is in building things that can't be generated by templates:
- Custom business logic and workflows
- System integrations
- SaaS products with recurring revenue
- Internal tools and dashboards
- MVPs for startup ideas

---

## Where These Clients Are

**Online communities:**
- Indie Hackers (indiehackers.com) — founders with ideas who can't build
- Product Hunt — startup community, launches daily
- Reddit: r/entrepreneur, r/SaaS, r/startups, r/smallbusiness
- Hacker News: "Who's hiring" and "Ask HN" threads

**Professional networks:**
- LinkedIn — startup founders, CTOs, ops managers with software problems
- AngelList / Wellfound — funded startups looking for dev help

**Direct:**
- Personal network — people with business ideas who need a builder
- Other agencies — looking for white-label dev partners
- Referrals from existing clients

---

## Content Strategy (what to post)

The goal is to be known as someone who builds real products, not just another freelancer.

**What works:**
- Show what you build (screenshots, demos, before/after)
- "I built X in 48 hours" posts — people love speed + quality
- Behind-the-scenes of using ship to build something
- Opinions on tools, frameworks, what actually works
- Case studies: "client had problem X, we built Y, result was Z"

**What doesn't work:**
- Generic "I'm a freelance developer available for work" posts
- Posting without showing any work
- Targeting everyone (no niche = no traction)

---

## Outreach Strategy

Cold email works when it's personal and relevant. Rules:

1. **Research first** — visit their site, understand their business before writing
2. **Lead with their problem** — not your skills
3. **Short emails** — 3-4 sentences max, one clear CTA
4. **Never mass blast** — 20 targeted emails beat 200 generic ones
5. **Follow up twice** — Day 4 and Day 7, then stop

Example angle for a startup founder:
> "Saw you're building [X]. Most founders at your stage get stuck on [specific technical problem].
> I build MVPs for exactly this — [specific relevant work].
> Worth a quick call?"
